Train Smarter. Play Stronger.

A simple, youth-friendly guide for safe, effective sports training.

Start with Warm-Ups

Warm-Ups

Warming up prepares your muscles, joints, and mind. Aim for 5–10 minutes of gentle movement before practice or games.

1) Light Jog + Arm Circles (2–3 min)

  • Easy jog or brisk walk.
  • 10 small and 10 large circles in each direction.

2) Dynamic Leg Swings (1–2 min)

  • Front-to-back and side-to-side, 10 each leg.
  • Hold something for balance if needed.

3) High Knees & Skips (2–3 min)

  • High knees for 20–30 seconds, rest, repeat.
  • Power skips focusing on tall posture.
Coach’s Tip: Warm-ups should feel easy to moderate – you should still be able to talk!

Stretching & Recovery

After training, cool down with slower movement and gentle, pain-free stretches to support flexibility and recovery.

Post-Training Stretches (Hold ~20–30s)

  • Calf stretch: Back heel flat, knee straight.
  • Quad stretch: Hold ankle behind you, knees close.
  • Hamstring stretch: Hinge at hips, long spine.
  • Hip flexor stretch: Half-kneel, tuck pelvis gently.

Recovery Habits

  • Hydrate and refuel with a balanced snack.
  • Prioritise sleep; growth happens during rest.
  • Alternate hard and easy days when possible.
  • Tell an adult or coach if something hurts.
Safety: Stretching should never be painful. Ease into positions and breathe slowly.

Motivation

Small, consistent habits beat giant, rare workouts. Use these ideas to build momentum:

Set Tiny Goals

Example: “5 minutes of warm-up every day after school.” Check off a simple habit tracker.

Make It Social

Train with friends, siblings, or teammates. Share progress and celebrate small wins.

Visual Reminders

Keep your shoes or ball by the door. Put your schedule somewhere you’ll see it daily.

Mindset: Replace “all or nothing” with “a little is better than nothing.”

Training Basics

Technique First

Master the movement slowly before going fast. Ask a coach or adult for feedback.

Progress Gradually

Increase time or difficulty little by little. Your body gets stronger with steady practice.

Balance & Variety

Mix skills: strength, speed, balance, and flexibility. Enjoy different drills and games.

Listen to Your Body

Stop if you feel sharp pain. Rest and talk to an adult or coach if something feels wrong.
